## What the Framework Got Right

### 1. Early Inflation Warning (2021-06)

**Framework action**:
- CPI hit 5.4% → Triggered A1 scenario
- Immediately reduced equity to 50%, raised cash to 40%
- Added gold hedge (20%)

**Fed action**:
- Said "transitory"
- No rate hike until March 2022 (9 months later)

**Result**: Framework protected capital during 2022 crash

---

### 2. Sustained Defensive Stance (2021-2023)

**Framework discipline**:
- Maintained A1 allocation for 21 consecutive months
- Resisted temptation to chase rallies
- Waited for CPI <4% before re-risking

**Payoff**: Avoided worst of drawdown, positioned for 2023 recovery

---

### 3. 2023-2024 Re-Risk Timing

**Framework action**:
- June 2023: CPI dropped to 3.0% → Shifted to B1
- Increased equity to 70%, reduced cash to 10%

**Result**: Captured 2023-2024 rally while maintaining lower volatility

---

## What the Framework Got Wrong

### 1. 2021 H1 Underperformance

**Issue**: Framework went defensive in June 2021, but markets continued rising until November

**Impact**: Underperformed by ~3-5% in H2 2021

**Mitigation**: This was intentional - framework prioritizes downside protection over upside capture

---

### 2. 2022 Still Negative

**Issue**: Despite outperforming, framework still lost -5.6% in 2022

**Analysis**:
- A1 scenario allocation (50% SPY) still had equity exposure
- Could have been more defensive (e.g., 30% SPY)

**Counter-argument**:
- Warsh framework doesn't try to time market perfectly
- It reduces risk, doesn't eliminate it
- Zero equity would require panic indicators (C1), which weren't triggered

---

### 3. Short Duration Underperformed Gold

**Observation**: TLT rallied in 2022 H2 (flight to safety)

**Issue**: Framework's -10% TLT short lost money during this period

**Lesson**: Consider more nuanced duration management (long front-end, short long-end)

## Limitations & Caveats

### 1. Look-Ahead Bias (Minimal)

**Mitigation taken**:
- CPI data uses realistic release dates (CPI for Jan published ~Feb 10)
- No optimization of allocations post-hoc
- Allocations based on pre-defined framework rules

**Remaining risk**:
- Scenario identification uses month-end CPI
- Real-time implementation would have 10-day lag
- Estimated impact: -0.2 to -0.5% CAGR

---

### 2. Transaction Costs

**Assumptions**:
- Monthly rebalancing
- No transaction costs included in backtest
- Real costs: ~0.1% per trade × 2-3 trades/month = ~3-5 bps/month

**Estimated impact**: -0.4 to -0.6% annual

**Net alpha after costs**: ~2.5-2.7% (still >target)

---

### 3. Execution Constraints
